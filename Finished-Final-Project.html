Summary of Analysis:

  • Title Repetition:
    • Repetition shows little correlation with year, song length, or chart rank, with most songs staying below 20 repetitions.
    • Disco and labels like Virgin and RSO have the highest repetition levels, while Soft rock has the lowest. Columbia shows the widest variation.
    • R&B and Pop/R&B contain the most extreme outliers, with some songs exceeding 40 title repetitions.
    • Most songs fall between 100–300 seconds and 0–20 title repetitions, making highly repetitive songs uncommon.
  • Line Repetition:
    • Pop, R&B, and Pop rock songs tend to have the most repetitive lyrics, while Country and Soft rock are generally less repetitive.
    • Record labels strongly influence repetition style: Columbia songs are the most repetitive, while Motown songs are the least.
    • Song length has only a weak relationship with repetition, meaning longer songs are not always more repetitive.
    • Lyric repetition has generally increased over time, but chart rank shows little connection to repetition levels.

Measuring Repetitions:

  • We ran two different types of repetition measurements; by phrase and by title. For each one, we made a function that would read a csv file of the song lyrics and count the number of times the title or an entire line would repeat throughout the song.
  • For measuring by title, the function checks that all required columns exist, because some songs don’t have lyrics, so it’ll just skip over those. Then it cleans/standardizes the text by making it lowercase and removing any punctuation. Then it counts title repetitions in the lyrics for each song, removing songs where the title appears only once or not at all. Finally it returns the filtered data set with the repetition counts included.
  • For measuring by phrase, the function separates the lyrics into individual lines, removing any blank lines that separated parts of the song. Then it counts how many times each lyric line repeats in a song, filtering out lines that only appear once, and sorts the songs by rank. Finally, it returns a data set showing the repeated lyric phrases and their counts.
